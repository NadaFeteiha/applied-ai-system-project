FISH CARE GUIDE
===============

TANK SETUP & WATER QUALITY
Tank size matters: the general rule is 1 inch of fish per gallon, but larger tanks are more stable and healthier. Freshwater tropical fish need water temperature between 74 and 82°F; goldfish prefer cooler water at 65 to 72°F. Use a reliable filter and allow 2 to 4 weeks for the nitrogen cycle to establish beneficial bacteria before adding fish. Test water weekly for ammonia (0 ppm), nitrite (0 ppm), nitrate (<20 ppm), and pH appropriate to your fish species. Perform 25 to 30% water changes weekly using a siphon to remove waste from the substrate.

FEEDING
Feed fish small amounts once or twice daily — only what they consume in 2 to 3 minutes. Overfeeding is the most common mistake and pollutes water. Use species-appropriate food: flakes for surface feeders, pellets for mid-water feeders, wafers or sinking pellets for bottom feeders. Supplement with frozen or live foods (bloodworms, brine shrimp) for variety. Fast fish one day per week to prevent digestive problems. Never leave uneaten food in the tank for more than a few minutes.

LIGHTING & ENVIRONMENT
Most tropical freshwater fish need 8 to 12 hours of light per day. Use a timer for consistency. Live plants benefit water quality by absorbing nitrates and providing hiding spots. Provide hiding places (caves, driftwood, plants) to reduce stress, especially for shy fish. Avoid putting the tank in direct sunlight which causes algae blooms.

SALTWATER/MARINE FISH
Saltwater tanks are more complex and expensive to maintain. Salinity should be kept at 1.024 to 1.026 specific gravity. Protein skimmers, live rock, and more frequent water testing are required. Reef tanks with corals have even stricter water parameter requirements. Not recommended for beginners.

COMMON FRESHWATER SPECIES AND CARE
- Betta Fish: Keep alone (males are aggressive), small tank acceptable (5+ gallons preferred), 76 to 82°F, change 25% of water weekly. Labyrinth fish that can breathe air.
- Goldfish: Coldwater, produce significant waste, need large tank (20+ gallons per fish), frequent water changes.
- Guppies: Hardy and colorful, easy for beginners, 72 to 82°F, keep in groups.
- Neon Tetras: Schooling fish, keep in groups of 6+, peaceful community fish, 70 to 81°F.
- Angelfish: Tall, elegant, semi-aggressive, need a tall tank, 76 to 84°F.
- Corydoras Catfish: Bottom feeders, peaceful, keep in groups of 4+, clean substrate debris.
- Plecostomus: Algae eater, grows large, needs hiding spots and driftwood.

HEALTH & DISEASE
Common fish diseases: ich (white spots), fin rot, velvet, dropsy, swim bladder disorder. Quarantine new fish for 2 to 4 weeks before adding to the main tank. Maintain good water quality to prevent most diseases. Treat sick fish in a separate hospital tank to avoid medicating beneficial bacteria in the main tank.

WHEN TO CONSULT A VET OR AQUATIC SPECIALIST
Most fish health issues are managed at home, but consult a specialist for: persistent disease outbreak despite treatment, unexplained mass die-off, unusual growths or lesions, and behavioral changes in valuable fish. Aquatic veterinarians can diagnose bacterial, viral, and parasitic conditions accurately.

SIGNS OF A HEALTHY FISH
- Active swimming and normal behavior for the species
- Clear eyes (not cloudy or protruding)
- Intact, undamaged fins
- Normal coloration (vibrant, not pale or blotchy)
- Eating regularly
- No unusual spots, growths, or mucus coating
