CAT CARE GUIDE
==============

FEEDING
Feed adult cats twice daily with high-quality wet or dry food formulated for cats. Cats are obligate carnivores — they require meat-based protein. Never feed cats dog food, onions, garlic, raw fish, alcohol, chocolate, or xylitol. Wet food provides additional hydration, which benefits kidney and urinary health. Kittens under 6 months need three to four small meals per day. Always provide fresh, clean water. Many cats prefer running water — a cat fountain can encourage drinking.

EXERCISE & ENRICHMENT
Indoor cats need 10 to 15 minutes of active play twice daily to prevent boredom and obesity. Use interactive toys (wand toys, laser pointers, puzzle feeders). Provide scratching posts and cat trees to satisfy natural instincts. Window perches give cats mental stimulation. Rotate toys to keep interest high. Without enrichment, cats may develop destructive behaviors or depression.

GROOMING
Short-haired cats need brushing once a week; long-haired cats (Persians, Maine Coons) need daily brushing. Trim nails every 2 to 3 weeks. Clean ears every 2 to 4 weeks if wax is visible. Cats are generally self-grooming but benefit from regular brushing to reduce hairballs. Bathe only if necessary; most cats dislike water and can maintain their own cleanliness. Brush teeth 2 to 3 times per week to prevent dental disease.

HEALTH & VACCINATIONS
Core vaccines: rabies, feline herpesvirus, calicivirus, and panleukopenia (FVRCP combo). Indoor cats still require vaccinations. Spay or neuter around 5 to 6 months of age. Annual wellness exams are recommended; senior cats (11+ years) should be seen twice yearly. Flea and tick prevention is important, especially for outdoor cats. Watch for signs of urinary issues — straining to urinate or blood in urine requires immediate vet attention.

LITTER BOX CARE
Provide one litter box per cat plus one extra. Scoop daily and fully change litter every 1 to 2 weeks. Place boxes in quiet, accessible locations. Cats may avoid dirty boxes and eliminate elsewhere. Changes in litter box habits (more or less frequent) can signal health issues.

WHEN TO VISIT A VET
Seek immediate vet care for: straining to urinate (especially male cats — can be life-threatening), vomiting more than 2 to 3 times per day, not eating for more than 24 to 48 hours, difficulty breathing, eye discharge or cloudiness, sudden weight loss, hiding and lethargy, or suspected injury.

INDOOR VS OUTDOOR CATS
Indoor cats live an average of 12 to 18 years; outdoor cats live 2 to 5 years on average due to dangers (cars, predators, disease). Indoor cats need more enrichment to stay mentally healthy. If allowing outdoor access, consider a catio or leash-training.

COMMON BREEDS AND THEIR NEEDS
- Persian: Long coat requires daily grooming, flat face prone to breathing issues, calm temperament.
- Maine Coon: Large breed, playful and dog-like, needs weekly brushing.
- Siamese: Vocal and social, needs lots of interaction.
- Bengal: Highly active, needs lots of play and stimulation.
- Ragdoll: Gentle and docile, good with children, moderate grooming.
