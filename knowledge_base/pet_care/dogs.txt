DOG CARE GUIDE
==============

FEEDING
Dogs should be fed twice daily — once in the morning and once in the evening. Puppies under 6 months need three to four meals per day. Use high-quality commercial dog food appropriate for the dog's age (puppy, adult, senior). Avoid feeding chocolate, grapes, raisins, onions, garlic, xylitol, macadamia nuts, and cooked bones as these are toxic to dogs. Always provide fresh water. Adjust portion size based on the dog's weight, age, and activity level.

EXERCISE
Adult dogs need at least 30 to 60 minutes of exercise per day. High-energy breeds (Border Collies, Huskies, Retrievers) may need 1 to 2 hours. Low-energy breeds (Bulldogs, Basset Hounds) need about 20 to 30 minutes. Puppies should exercise in short bursts to protect growing joints — about 5 minutes per month of age twice daily. Senior dogs still need daily walks but at a slower pace. Exercise prevents obesity, reduces anxiety, and supports joint health.

GROOMING
Brush your dog at least once a week; double-coated breeds (Huskies, Collies) should be brushed 2 to 3 times per week. Bathe your dog every 4 to 6 weeks or when visibly dirty. Trim nails every 3 to 4 weeks — overgrown nails cause pain and gait problems. Clean ears weekly with a dog-safe ear cleaner to prevent infections. Brush teeth daily or at least 3 times per week to prevent dental disease.

HEALTH & VACCINATIONS
Core vaccines include distemper, parvovirus, adenovirus, and rabies. Puppies need a series of vaccines starting at 6 to 8 weeks old. Annual or triennial boosters are required throughout life. Heartworm prevention medication should be given monthly year-round. Flea and tick prevention is essential, especially in warmer months. Annual vet checkups are recommended; senior dogs (7+ years) benefit from twice-yearly checkups.

SOCIALIZATION & TRAINING
Socialize puppies between 3 and 14 weeks — expose them to people, other dogs, sounds, and environments. Basic obedience training (sit, stay, come, down) builds a safe and well-behaved companion. Positive reinforcement (treats, praise) is the most effective training method. Dogs are social animals and should not be left alone for more than 6 to 8 hours.

WHEN TO VISIT A VET
Schedule a vet visit immediately if your dog shows: vomiting or diarrhea lasting more than 24 hours, difficulty breathing, collapse or extreme lethargy, seizures, suspected poisoning, wounds that won't stop bleeding, eye injuries, inability to urinate, swollen abdomen, or signs of severe pain.

COMMON BREEDS AND THEIR NEEDS
- Labrador Retriever: High energy, needs 1+ hour of exercise daily, prone to obesity and hip dysplasia.
- Golden Retriever: Friendly, needs 1 hour of exercise daily, regular grooming required.
- German Shepherd: Intelligent and active, needs 2 hours of exercise daily, weekly brushing.
- Poodle: Low-shedding, needs professional grooming every 6 to 8 weeks, highly intelligent.
- Chihuahua: Small dog, 20 to 30 minutes exercise daily, sensitive to cold.
- Bulldog: Low energy, 20 to 30 minutes light exercise, prone to breathing issues.
