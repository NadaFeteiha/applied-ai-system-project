EDUCATION & EXPERIENCE
Dr. Lisa Thompson completed her DVM at Michigan State University College of Veterinary Medicine and pursued advanced training through the Royal College of Veterinary Surgeons (RCVS) Certificate in Advanced Veterinary Practice, Zoological Medicine track. She has 10 years of hands-on experience exclusively with birds and small exotic mammals. She has given talks at international avian veterinary conferences and regularly collaborates with local wildlife rescue organizations.

ABOUT DR. THOMPSON
Dr. Thompson has dedicated her career to the animals most vets are not trained to treat. She understands the unique physiology and health needs of birds and small mammals, many of which require completely different diagnostic and treatment approaches compared to dogs and cats. Her calm handling technique minimizes stress for patients that can go into shock easily. The clinic is specifically designed for avian and small mammal comfort — including species-appropriate waiting areas with reduced stress layouts.

IMPORTANT NOTES FOR BIRD OWNERS
- Birds must be transported in a covered, secure carrier
- Keep the carrier warm during transport (birds are susceptible to cold stress)
- Do not clean the cage before the appointment — fresh droppings may be needed for diagnostics
- Bring a sample of the normal diet and note any recent changes in diet or behavior
- Mention any household chemicals, air fresheners, candles, or cooking fumes the bird may have been exposed to

IMPORTANT NOTES FOR SMALL MAMMAL OWNERS
- Unlike dogs and cats, rabbits should NOT be fasted before most procedures
- Bring a small amount of familiar food to help the pet stay calm during the visit
- Rabbits and guinea pigs are prey animals — keep them away from other species in the waiting room
- Note any changes in droppings, eating, or activity in the past week
