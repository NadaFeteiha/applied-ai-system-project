EDUCATION & EXPERIENCE
Dr. Omar Hassan earned his DVM from Colorado State University and completed a 3-year residency in Emergency and Critical Care at BluePearl Veterinary Partners in New York. He is a Diplomate of the American College of Veterinary Emergency and Critical Care (DACVECC). He has 11 years of emergency medicine experience and has managed thousands of critical cases ranging from toxin ingestion and hit-by-car trauma to respiratory collapse and septic shock. He leads a team of 4 emergency veterinarians and 12 veterinary technicians at the 24-hour center.

WHEN TO GO TO THE EMERGENCY CENTER
Seek emergency care immediately for any of the following:
- Difficulty breathing or open-mouth breathing (cats)
- Suspected poisoning (known or unknown toxin ingestion)
- Trauma (hit by car, fall from height, animal attack)
- Uncontrolled bleeding
- Seizures lasting more than 2 minutes or repeated seizures
- Collapse, loss of consciousness, or unresponsiveness
- Inability to urinate (especially male cats — this is a life-threatening emergency)
- Suspected broken bones
- Eye injuries or sudden loss of vision
- Bloated or distended abdomen with retching (dogs — possible GDV/bloat, life-threatening)
- Severe vomiting or diarrhea with blood
- Extreme lethargy or unresponsiveness
- Birthing complications
- Suspected foreign body ingestion
